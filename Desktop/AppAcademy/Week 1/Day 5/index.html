    Ingredients

    • 1 tablespoon butter
    • 1 (6 ounce) bouneless pork loin chop, cut into small pieces
    • 1/4 cup chopped carrot
    • 1/4 cup chopped broccoli
    • 1 green onion, chopped
    • 1 egg, beaten
    • 1 cup cooked rice
    • 1/4 cup frozen peas
    • 1 1/2 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 1/8 teaspoon garlic powder
    • 1/8 teaspoon ground ginger

    Fried Rice

    Description

    Fried rice is a popular street food in Asia. In some Asian countries, small restaurants, street vendors and traveling hawkers specialize in serving fried rice. In Indonesian cities it is common to find fried rice street hawkers moving through the streets with their food cart and stationing it in busy streets or residential areas.

    Directions

    1. Melt butter in a large non-stick skillet over medium heat. Cook and stir pork, carrot, broccoli, peas, and green onion in melted butter until pork is cooked through, 7 to 10 minutes. Remove pork mixture to a bowl and return skillet to medium heat.
    2. Scramble egg in the skillet until completely set. Return the pork mixture to the skillet. Stir rice, peas, soy sauce, garlic powder, and ground ginger into the pork mixture; cook and stir until heated through, 7 to 10 minutes.
